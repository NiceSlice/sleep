How to sleep better?


Here are some things you can change about your sleep habits to ensure better rest.

Turn off lights

Artificial light in the evenings will affect your internal sleep-wake cycle. Your body then suppresses the melatonin release making it harder for you to fall asleep. For this reason, it's best to stay away from the screens before sleep.

Lay off drinking

It may seem that alcohol helps you fall asleep more easily but it actually disrupts your sleep. Alcohol-infused sleep is fragmented, full of brief awakenings. Alcohol is also one of the most powerful suppressors of REM sleep. For good quality sleep stay sober.

Cool the room

To successfully initiate sleep your core temperature needs to decrease by about 1 degree Celsius. For this reason it's easier to fall asleep in a room that's too cold than one that's too hot.
